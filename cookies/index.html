Cookies are data, stored in small text files, on your computer. On the request of a webserver, your browser creates such a file. After this happens, the webserver can read and write content from and to this file. Once the cookie has been read by the code on the server or the client computer, the data can be retrieved and used to customize the web the web page appropriately. Please open in firefox, it works. Chrome has a slightly different interpretation of the same-origin policy compared to Firefox or Internet Explorer, particularly when it comes to the file:/// protocol.
